White and blue porcelain from Jingdezhen.

Transcultural Design, 2018 — Transcultural approach of a Chinese ancestral know-how.

Very interested in Chinese culture, I decided to spend a month in Jingdezhen in China, the world capital of porcelain. I was hosted by The Opposite Studio and I was able to discover the manufacturing process of this ancestral Chinese know-how. The Opposite Studio also welcomes artists from all over the world for residencies. I met artists from Japan, France, the United States, Korea, and of course China. Working with them allowed me to see that it was possible to have another approach to ceramics. It is therefore with this transcultural approach that I decided to create some pieces myself by painting traditional Chinese vases, with my own graphic style. The approach here is to mix two cultures. Being a foreigner, and discovering Chinese culture, the goal is not to copy the works of this country, but to adapt them with my personal approach.

Skills

• Transcultural design — adaptation of two cultures
• Manual work — experience a Chinese know-how
• Graphic design — develop a graphic identity

Jingdezhen is a completely different city from Shanghai. This trip allowed me to immerse myself in the Chinese community and to participate in the creation of this famous Chinese porcelain.
